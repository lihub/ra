The risk that the entire market will decline, affecting the value of individual securities regardless of their specific performance. Market downturns can result in significant portfolio losses.

  • Economic recessions and market corrections
  • Interest rate changes affecting bond and equity values
  • Geopolitical events and policy changes
  • Inflation and currency fluctuation impacts

Volatility Risk

Investment values can fluctuate significantly over short periods. Higher potential returns generally come with higher volatility and greater risk of loss.

  • Daily price fluctuations in equity markets
  • Interest rate sensitivity in bond investments
  • Currency exchange rate volatility for international assets
  • Commodity price swings affecting related investments

Liquidity Risk

The risk that investments cannot be sold quickly at fair market value. Some assets may be difficult to sell during market stress periods.

  • Limited trading volume in certain securities
  • Market disruptions affecting normal trading
  • Emergency redemption restrictions during crises
  • Bid-ask spreads widening during volatile periods

Credit Risk

The risk that bond issuers may default on their obligations, potentially resulting in partial or total loss of invested principal and interest.

  • Corporate bankruptcy and bond defaults
  • Government debt crises and sovereign defaults
  • Credit rating downgrades affecting bond values
  • Counterparty risk in derivative instruments

Inflation Risk

The risk that inflation will reduce the purchasing power of your investment returns over time, particularly affecting fixed-income investments.

  • Rising prices eroding real returns
  • Fixed-rate bonds losing value during inflation
  • Currency devaluation in international investments
  • Cost of living increases outpacing investment gains

Concentration Risk

Despite diversification efforts, portfolios may become concentrated in certain sectors, geographies, or asset classes, increasing risk exposure.

  • Over-allocation to specific market sectors
  • Geographic concentration despite international diversification
  • Currency concentration in USD-denominated assets
  • Factor concentration in growth or value strategies
